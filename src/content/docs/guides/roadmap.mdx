KSP2 Redux is following a multi-stage development plan to bring the promised features of KSP2 to life. Below is our complete roadmap.

![Roadmap](/roadmap.png)

<Aside type="note">
This roadmap is subject to change as the project evolves based on technical discoveries and community feedback.
</Aside>

## Stage 0: Foundation

> *"We choose to go to space, not because it is easy, but because we thought it would be easy."*

The "0 Days Since Last Accident" sign at KSC once again resets to zero. After too many rapid unscheduled disassemblies, the Kerbals decide it's time to look under the hood and start making rockets that don't explode… that much.

The initial series of Redux releases will be focusing on building a solid foundation for the game going forward.

### Performance & Fixes

<CardGrid>
<Card title="Optimizations" icon="rocket">
- Optimizations of existing game systems and graphics settings to increase performance
- Fixes for various game bugs and inconsistencies, big and small
- Conversion of core simulation code to use Unity's **DOTS** systems for massive performance gains
</Card>

<Card title="Test Results" icon="approve-check">
- Small vessels: 50 FPS → 78 FPS (56% improvement)
- 200 parts: 18 FPS → 24 FPS (33% improvement)  
- 1500 parts: 638ms/frame → 347ms/frame (46% reduction)
</Card>
</CardGrid>

### Vessel ISRU

- Small-scale preview of the larger resource management system, similar to KSP1's ISRU
- Vessel parts for scanning, gathering and processing resources necessary for basic refueling operations
- Production of Hydrogen, Methane, Oxygen and Monopropellant depending on the given location's available raw resources

### Heat Management

- Heat generation and dissipation system for vessel parts
- Reactors and drills will generate heat, radiators will remove it
- Introduction of radiator parts

### Tech Tree Updates

- **New requirement types** for unlocking tech tree nodes in addition to science points
- **Mission unlocks:** Require completing a specific mission before a tech node can be unlocked
- **Science experiment unlocks:** Require completion of specific experiments, sometimes in specific locations or biomes

### Modding SDK

- Improved modding experience fully integrated into the Unity Editor
- Possibility to run the game and test mods directly in Unity's play mode
- Multiple mods sharing a single Unity project to decrease total disk space

## Stage 1: Colonies & Resources

> *"I came, I saw, I built a colony… and then I ran out of coffee!"*

Kerbal scientists just had an epiphany! While "MOAR BOOSTERS" is always a viable and well-respected strategy, their latest coffee-stained blueprint (and yet another failed attempt at an Eve mission) sparked a wild idea. What if they could build the return vehicle directly on Eve? Just make sure Jeb doesn't lick the purple rocks again.

This stage will implement the core gameplay elements of colonies and the full resource system, as well as a brand-new game mode. This is complex, so it's split into 3 parts.

<Aside>
**Note:** Stages 1.1 and 1.2 may happen in reverse order.
</Aside>

### Stage 1.1: Planetary Colonies

#### Planetary Colonies
- Introduction of surface-based colony parts
- Resource gathering facilities and factories
- System for founding and building colonies using the Building Assembly Editor
- New raw resources and building materials
- Colony-based vessel factories and launch pads

#### Frontier Mode
- Initial limited-scale preview of a new game mode based on resource management
- All parts cost building materials which the player creates by processing raw resources gathered at colonies
- KSC has unlimited amounts of basic building resources and fuels
- Colonies and more exotic resources required to build advanced vessel and colony parts

<Aside type="tip">
**Example:** Redux might include a simple ISRU for colonies but mods could add many more of varying size, style, etc.
</Aside>

#### Colony Management UI
- Basic UI for tracking flow rates of all colony resources
- Electric charge generation and consumption monitoring
- Heat generation and dissipation tracking

### Stage 1.2: Orbital Colonies

#### Orbital Colonies
- New parts and systems for orbital colonies
- On-orbit vessel construction using orbital colonies as shipyards
- In-depth planning tools for EC generation and consumption

#### Exploration Updates
- New resource unlocking system for Exploration Mode (distinct from Frontier Mode)
- Resources are not continuously mined and used up
- Once requirements are met (e.g., through a dedicated mining facility), that resource is fully unlocked at that specific colony
- **Orbital colonies serve a special purpose:** Any two celestial bodies with active orbital colonies will share all their unlocked resources
- Create an interconnected network of colonies to make all unlocked resources available everywhere

### Stage 1.3: Supply Routes & Environment

#### Supply Routes
- Frontier Mode feature allowing automated resource and material supply routes between colonies
- Flying a mission from one colony to another gives option to record the flight as a supply route
- Periodically transfers the amount of resources the vessel was carrying from origin to destination
- Subtracts material costs of the supply vessel from the origin colony

#### Environmental Heat System
- Environments play a role in heat generation and dissipation
- Colonies might use water sources as coolant
- Must dissipate enough heat to compensate for hot biomes (e.g., lava rivers)
- In-depth planning tools for heat generation and dissipation

#### New Content
- Advanced engines well suited for hauling large quantities of resources
- New missions and science experiments related to colonies
- Additional content to be detailed later

## Stage 2: Interstellar

> *"To infinity… and probably a crash landing!"*

As the Kerbal civilization expands all across the Kerbol system, from the fiery pits of Moho to the freezing far reaches of the outer planets, new resources and scientific discoveries finally enable the Kerbals to travel to the stars. Excited, they blast off for distant systems - but what is this? Oh boy, it's going to be a long ride when Bob packed only decaf for the trip!

### Debdeb System

- **New star system**, the closest one to Kerbol
- Planets and moons which present new challenges
- Exotic resources and new building materials allowing creation of even more advanced vessels and colonies
- New missions and science experiments related to the new star system

### Interstellar Parts

- Massive parts for constructing vessels for interstellar travel
- Advanced far future types of propulsion enabling travel speeds at **significant fractions of speed of light**

## Stage 3: Multiplayer

> *"All for one, and one for all… unless we're fighting over the last donut!"*

One Kerbal crashing is fun, but a whole squad fighting over who gets to press the big red launch button? That's what we call Kerbal mayhem! Teaming up to build rockets, or "accidentally" nuking each other's bases, the Kerbals are buzzing with caffeine and chaos. Multiplayer makes the galaxy feel much less empty.

### Multiplayer Support

- Multiple players can share an agency
- Work together towards common goals
- Shared progress as players explore the galaxy
- Collaborative colony building and resource management
